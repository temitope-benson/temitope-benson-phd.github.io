Research Experience

Computational Biologist / MCDB Graduate Research Associate - Dawes Lab [2017-present]

Molecular, Cellular, and Developmental Biology Program; Department of Mathematics; Department of Molecular Genetics - the Ohio State University

Mathematical and Biological Exploration of Centrosome Maturation Dynamics Shows Novel Asymmetry Leading to Proper Centrosome Positioning [2018-present]

  • Developed a whole-cell ODE model to simulate centrosome maturation. Acquired and analyzed over 100 high-quality 4D fluorescent microscopy images, which were then used for model fitting.
  • Implemented a novel parameter search algorithm to efficiently explore parameter space and find a wider array of suitable parameter values.
  • Analysis of the parameter space revealed that the model captured the dynamics of three biological hypotheses, thus giving them a novel mathematical understanding and connection.
  • Used appropriate statistical tests to show that our in vivo data did indeed show a significant difference between centrosomes.

Double Strand Breaks (DSBs) are Required for Proper Crossover Positioning in a Reaction-Diffusion Model. [2018-2019]

  • For the proper patterning of crossover positions, the PDE Turing model requires the additional information provided by the localization pattern of DSBs. I coupled two such systems of equations together to position crossovers based on the patterns in the DSB model.
  • The mathematical model shows that a reaction-diffusion system can't simulate this process without incorporating double-strand break information.

Surface Patterning of Morphogens Coupled with Domain Force can Simulate Oscillations of Regenerating Hydra vulgaris Cellular Aggregates. [2017-2018]

  • Extending the PDE model described below of pollen aperture surface patterning, I could simulate the growth of Hydra aggregates during initial oscillations.
